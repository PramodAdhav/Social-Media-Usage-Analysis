Gender Distribution

A very simple conclusion that is drawn from the above pie chart is that the majority of them using social media are males. Can there be any factor that be drawn out from the statistical insights which makes males more prone towards social media usage?

Age Distribution

The inferences that can be drawn from this graph are that, majority of people using social media are between the age group of 15-25. So, why is that this age group is more exposed to the social media, compared to the others, what is it that attracts this age group particularly towards social media, is there any common factor apart from the age, that puts them together in this inference?

Most Used Social Media Platforms

Number of respondents majorly use Instagram, and being Instagram users ourselves, it is natural that people scroll reels, or post content. Scrolling reels is a source of entertainment, however if content makers, Instagram is a source of income. Setting Instagram apart, it can be observed that WhatsApp, is the second most used app with more than 40 percent of them using WhatsApp. So, can it be concluded that most of the social media users, use social media to connect with people or as a source of entertainment/income?

Time of Day Usage Across Social Media Platforms

It is a very simple graph that concludes that majority of them use social media either in the evenings or late nights.

Distribution of Importance of Social Media in Daily Life

Importance level of 5, for the social media is observed from more than 35 percent of respondents, and there are hardly 4 percent of respondents for whom social media doesn’t play a huge role in their lives. Considering the majority, social media is of great importance amongst the young generations. However, what is it that people gain from social media? Why is it so important for them? Is it potentially a catalyst for their growth and development? Or is it just a source of entertainment?

Relationship between Age and Confidence in Controlling Social Media Usage

From this graph it can be observed that a maximum number of people are quite confident in being able to control their social media usage, and this confidence increases with growing age. So, does age influence our control on usage of social media?

Sleep Impact Due to Social Media by Gender

Males and females, both the gender, believe that usage of social media has impacted the sleep routine, which implies that majority of them use social media at nights, most probably because everyone’s busy during the day time, and at nights they use social media, so is it safe to say, social media might not just be a source of entertainment/income but also acts as a distraction and can cause health issues as well?

Frequency of Content Posting by Most Used Platform

It can be inferred from this graph that majority of respondents do not post any content on social media, and even if they do its mostly Instagram or WhatsApp. So, from this and as well graphs and their inferences is it safe to assume that social media usage can be controlled to an extent if we could provide a way to control the usage of Instagram and WhatsApp?

Pressure to Present a Certain Image on Social Media by Age

Quite a few interesting inferences can be drawn from this graph, people are equally not sure and feel pressured to present a particular image on social media. So, in particularly no sure conclusion can be drawn with naked graphs unless we perform some statistical tactics on these graphs.

Primary Use of Social Media by Gender

This graph provides us with a data driven conclusion that majority of people use social media platform to stay connected with people or use social media to text. And as well use social media to discover content.

Frequency of Social Media Usage per Day

Considering this graph it can be observed that a majority of people use social media for more than ten times a day, at the same time we can observe that approximately 15 percent of the respondents use social media for just 1-3 times a day. So, what sets apart these 15 percent of respondents is that scrolling through social media, isn’t typically a part of their daily routine. However what matters to us is what is it that keeps them busy and not let them use social media, and can this be related to the age of the respondents? Their job?

Frequency of Interacting with New People by Platform

From this graph it is observed that people rarely encounter new interactions, its hardly a few times in a week. So, what is it the keeps people engaged in a familiar content every single day? And why Instagram and WhatsApp again? And not any other social media app?
