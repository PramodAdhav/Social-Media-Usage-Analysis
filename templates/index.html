Welcome to InsightWave

We've developed a web application for analyzing social media usage and psychological behavior, gathering insights from 120 respondents via Google Sheets. The backend, powered by Flask and MSSQL, efficiently processes queries and generates charts and graphs. HTML and CSS ensure a user-friendly frontend interface, while pyodbc facilitates seamless database connectivity. Data visualization is achieved through Matplotlib, Seaborn, and Pandas Pyplot, offering compelling insights into social media trends. Our project provides a valuable tool for researchers and individuals keen on understanding the dynamic interplay between social media usage and psychological factors.

Gender Distribution

Among the 120 respondents, 68 are male, which is 56.7% of the total. This suggests that males are more active or more likely to respond to social media usage surveys. The remaining 52 respondents are female, making up 43.3% of the total. This indicates a strong presence of female users, although slightly less than males. While the gender distribution is fairly balanced, the slight male majority could introduce a slight bias in the overall analysis of social media usage habits

Age Distribution

The majority of respondents are clustered around the age of 20, indicating that young adults are the predominant age group using social media in this survey. There is a noticeable peak around the age range of approximately 18 to 25 years. The distribution extends up to around 70 years, but the frequency of respondents in the older age groups is significantly lower. The black bar in the center represents the interquartile range (IQR), which shows the middle 50% of the data. The data suggests that social media platforms and marketers should focus on the younger demographic, particularly those around 20 years old.

Most Used Social Media Platforms

Instagram is the most popular platform among the respondents, with nearly 70 users indicating it as their preferred social media platform. This suggests a strong preference for Instagram, likely due to its visual content focus and popularity among younger demographics. WhatsApp is the second most popular platform, with around 40 respondents using it. This indicates a significant number of users prefer this messaging app for communication. YouTube has a smaller user base among the respondents, with fewer than 10 indicating it as their preferred platform. This could suggest that while YouTube is widely used for video content, it might not be the primary social media choice for this sample.

Time of Day Usage Across Social Media Platforms

The bar graph represents the number of users on various social media platforms at different times of day: Morning, Afternoon, Evening, and Late Night.
Here are some observations:
Facebook: The highest usage is in the evening, followed by the afternoon. Late-night usage is relatively low.
WhatsApp: Usage is evenly distributed across all times of day.
Instagram: Usage peaks in the afternoon and evening, with a dip in the morning.
LinkedIn: Most users are active during the afternoon and evening.
Reddit: Usage is consistent throughout the day, with a slight increase in the evening.
Twitter: The highest activity is during the afternoon, followed by the evening.
YouTube: The platform sees the most engagement in the evening.

Distribution of Importance of Social Media in Daily Life

The line graph represents the number of respondents who rated different levels of importance. Respondents rated different importance levels, with the following observations: Importance Level 1 had just below 10 respondents; Importance Levels 3-4 were rated by around 5 respondents; Importance Level 5 peaked with nearly 17.5 respondents; Importance Levels 6-7 fluctuated; Importance Level 8 had about 15 respondents; and Importance Level 10 ended just above 10 respondents.

Relationship between Age and Confidence in Controlling Social Media Usage

The scatter plot graph depicts the relationship between Age (on the horizontal axis) and Confidence in Controlling (on the vertical axis). Here are the key observations: Younger Age Group: There’s a cluster of data points at the lower age range (around 10 to 20 years) with high confidence levels in contrrolling their usage. As Age Increases, the number of data points decreases, and there’s a slight trend towards lower confidence levels. The graph suggests that younger individuals may have higher confidence compared to older individuals.

Sleep Impact Due to Social Media by Gender

Males and females, both the gender, believe that usage of social media has impacted the sleep routine, which implies that majority of them use social media at nights, most probably because everyone’s busy during the day time, and at nights they use social media.

Pressure to Present a Certain Image on Social Media by Age

Quite a few interesting inferences can be drawn from this graph, people are equally not sure and feel pressured to present a particular image on social media. Around 20 respondents feel that they are comfortable in being genuine on social media platforms.

Primary Use of Social Media by Gender

This graph provides us with a data driven conclusion that majority of people use social media platform to stay connected with people or use social media to text. And as well use social media to discover content.

Frequency of Social Media Usage per Day

Considering this graph it can be observed that a majority of people use social media for more than ten times a day, at the same time we can observe that approximately 15 percent of the respondents use social media for just 1-3 times a day. So, what sets apart these 15 percent of respondents is that scrolling through social media, isn’t typically a part of their daily routine.

Frequency of Interacting with New People by Platform

From this graph it is observed that people rarely encounter new interactions, its hardly a few times in a week. Around 35-40 respondents do not or rarely interact with new people. And when it comes to Whatsapp, only 5-6 people interact with new people. There is no much interaction by the respondents on any other platforms.
